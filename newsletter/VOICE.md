# The District Angels voice

How the newsletter should sound. Distilled from the website and how we talk about the community. When drafting, err on the side of sounding like a person writing to friends, not an institution writing to a list.

## Who's talking, to whom

A volunteer leader of the community writing to members — angels, fellows, founders, partners. Warm, direct, first person plural ("we"), occasionally first person singular for the opener. The reader is a peer, not an audience.

## The core attitude

- **Capital-region pride, zero defensiveness.** The DMV has the talent, the institutions, and the money — we're the ones connecting them. Say it plainly.
- **Investing should be easy, fun, and rewarding.** Energy over gravitas. This is a community people *want* to be part of, not a chore.
- **Open and curated.** Everyone's invited to the community; the right people end up in the right rooms. Both halves matter.
- **Volunteer-led and proud of it.** People show up because they care. Thank them by name.
- **We back what's next.** Forward-looking, builder-friendly, allergic to cynicism.

## How it reads

- **Short sentences. Concrete details.** "Checks start at $2.5K" beats "accessible minimums."
- **Plain words over finance-speak.** "Put money into" not "deploy capital into." Exception: real terms members need (SPV, co-invest) — use them without over-explaining.
- **Names and shoutouts.** Fellows who wrote reports, members who sourced deals, partners who hosted. Named credit is a feature of every issue.
- **Specific asks.** Never "get involved!" — always "we need two angels with health-tech backgrounds to join the diligence call Thursday."
- **Light humor, no forced jokes.** A wry aside is fine; puns and hype-speak are not.
- **Sentence case headings.** No ALL CAPS, minimal exclamation points (one per issue, max).

## Words we use

back what's next · the capital region / the DMV · community · angels · fellows (Halo Fellows) · founders anywhere, investors in the capital region · co-invest · in the room · open & curated · volunteer-led

## Words we avoid

synergy · leverage (as a verb) · ecosystem play · thrilled/delighted/excited to announce (just announce it) · game-changing · disruptive · "don't miss out" urgency-bait

## Newsletter anatomy

Standard section order (skip any that are empty this month):

1. **Opener** — 2–4 sentences from the writer, personal, sets the tone. What's on our mind this month.
2. **Upcoming events** — what, when, where, one line on why to come, RSVP link.
3. **Deal corner** — active raises and recently closed SPVs. Only founder-approved info. Clear CTA: "reply if you want the memo."
4. **Community asks** — specific requests from members and founders. Who needs what, how to help.
5. **From the fellows** — new reports, research, fellow milestones. Link to the news page.
6. **Wins & shoutouts** — portfolio news, member wins, thank-yous. End warm.

Subject lines: concrete and curiosity-driven, under 50 characters, no clickbait. Good: "Two raises, a rooftop, and a robot report." Bad: "You won't BELIEVE this month's update!!"
