# DA Team Playbook — every flow in numbered steps

Companion to [`OPERATING-SYSTEM.md`](OPERATING-SYSTEM.md) (that's the *why*; this is the *how*). **You** = a human does it. **Auto** = it happens on its own.

> **The one habit that powers everything:** when you learn something about a person, a deal, or a sponsor — paste a paragraph in **#da-brain** and react ✅. Every automation below feeds off that database.

## People & community

**1 · New member intake**
1. Prospect fills the short Tally form (5 fields).
2. Auto: redirected straight to the booking page — most book on the spot.
3. Auto: Brain record created, Slack alert, booking-link email sent.
4. Auto: no booking → nudges day 2, 5, 10; then newsletter-only.
5. You: take the intro call (auto-recorded → tags fill their profile).
6. Auto: onboarding fires — welcome email, Luma calendar, Slack invite.

**2 · Adding anyone to the Brain**
1. You: paste one paragraph about them in **#da-brain**.
2. Auto: Claude replies in-thread with a structured contact card.
3. You: react ✅ on your original message (or reply with a correction).
4. Auto: card commits to the database; bot confirms.

**3 · Luma RSVPs into the Brain** *(the only manual port left — 60 seconds)*
1. Event runs on Luma (free) as always.
2. You: after the event, export the guest CSV from Luma.
3. You: drop it in the **Luma Imports** Drive folder, named `<event> YYYY-MM-DD.csv`.
4. Auto: new contacts created, attendance logged, Slack summary posted.

**4 · Earning & spending points**
1. Auto: actions log themselves — deals brought, referrals, connects, deal feedback, attendance.
2. Deal we invest in = next year's fee waived. Referral = 100. Connect that converts = 150. Feedback = 25.
3. Auto: 500 pts flags a fee waiver at renewal; 250 unlocks an exclusive-event invite.
4. Top decile → Charter invitation + gala awards. Points expire after 24 months.

## Deals & investing

**5 · Deal flow, end to end**
1. Founder applies via the intake form (or referral — referrer logged).
2. You: two-person rubric screen; pass email drafted for you if it's a no.
3. Advance → founder presents at the monthly Deal Room.
4. Auto: diligence opens — portal page + memo skeleton created.
5. Members drop questions; agent dedupes them into the founder agenda.
6. Auto: experts matched from the Brain; calls recorded, notes extracted.
7. You: complete financial diligence, terms, dilution math (hard gate).
8. IC (3–5 members) scores the memo: OK / not OK / more info.
9. OK → SPV opens on Play Money; commitments and doc status tracked with nudges.
10. Close → founder enters the portfolio loop.

**6 · The 2-page memo**
1. Auto: deal enters diligence → all 9 memo sections created as open items.
2. Auto: agent pre-fills from founder form, transcripts, expert notes.
3. You (deal lead): verify and complete each section — bot nudges the empty ones.
4. Page 2 is non-negotiable: verified revenue (or bank-verified runway), terms, dilution through the next two rounds.
5. No IC vote until every section is filled or explicitly waived.

**7 · Portfolio loop**
1. Auto: founder gets the quarterly 5-minute update form, reminded until submitted.
2. Auto: update parsed into the Brain; runway < 6 months or 2 misses → flagged to #deals.
3. Auto: their asks matched against the network → intro drafts.
4. You: approve the intros; every one lands in the impact ledger.
5. Auto: perks scheduled — demo slot, newsletter feature, office hours.
6. Auto: quarterly per-company impact report compiles itself.

**8 · Participating in a deal (members)**
1. Touch 1: Deal Room invite.
2. Touch 2: diligence summary + interest ask — one click: Committed / Interested / Watching / Pass (+why).
3. Interested+ get portal thread digests; everyone else is left alone.
4. Touch 3: SPV opens — commit on Play Money; unsigned docs get nudged.
5. Feedback and pass-reasons earn points.

## Events & money

**9 · Running an event**
1. T-21: event created from template in Luma; speaker confirmed; sponsor checklist attached.
2. T-14: agent drafts LinkedIn + Luma + email from one description; approve in Slack → scheduled.
3. T-7 / T-1: auto — pushes, reminders, run-of-show, sponsor deliverables check.
4. T+1: CSV drop (flow 3); agent drafts recap + thank-yous; approve & send.
5. T+3: auto — contacts tagged, no-shows get the recap, engagement scores update.

**10 · Angel happy hour curation** *(bimonthly, invite-only, cap 30–40)*
1. Auto: agent proposes the list — ⅓ anchor members, ⅓ hottest prospects, ⅓ member guests.
2. You: approve/adjust; invites go out via a private Luma event.
3. Members bring one guest each (accredited or strategic — earns points).
4. No pitching; sector mix enforced; non-converters rotate out after two invites.
5. Auto: 60-day conversion tracked — did attendees join or mark first deal interest?

**11 · Sponsors**
1. You: pick targets from the sponsor map → outreach pipeline (flow 12).
2. Signed → package recorded: cash + in-kind value, benefits owed, renewal date.
3. Auto: before each event, bot posts exactly what that sponsor is owed.
4. Auto: after, proof compiles — attendance, photos, mentions, leads.
5. Renewal conversation starts from receipts, not recollection.

**12 · Outreach (any persona)**
1. You: define the campaign + target list.
2. Auto: Brain checked for a warm path first — member intro beats cold.
3. Cold → agent drafts a personalized email; batch-approve in Slack.
4. Auto: sent + logged; any reply flips the stage and pings Slack.
5. Auto: no reply → follow-ups day 4 and day 9, then closed.
6. Auto: weekly digest — sent / replied / meetings / won, per campaign.

**13 · Grants**
1. You: identify the funder → pipeline: LOI → application → awarded → reporting.
2. Auto: deadlines become open items; nothing slips.
3. Applications cite the live numbers: investors activated, capital deployed, founders served, jobs.
4. Auto: quarterly Ecosystem Impact Report drafts itself — same doc serves sponsors and embassies.

## Comms & rhythm

**14 · Newsletter**
1. Everyone, all month: post updates in **#da-internal-newsletters** — a sentence is enough; drop images right in the channel.
2. Auto: everything mirrors to the inbox doc + Drive.
3. Last Monday: run **/newsletter** → full draft in DA voice.
4. You: edit in plain English; render to the Mailchimp template.
5. Paste into Mailchimp → send Thursday. Next month starts clean automatically.

**15 · The weekly rhythm**
1. Mon (auto): open-items digest posts to #ops; MD brief auto-drafts from the week.
2. Tue: Naviya + Blaire edit the brief (~20 minutes).
3. Wed: MD walkthrough; decisions logged back as open items with owners.
4. Thu (auto): second digest; stuck items escalate by age.
5. Volunteers: one async Slack thread a week — wins flow into the brief and newsletter automatically. No sheet.

**16 · Open items — nothing drowns**
1. Auto: tasks extract themselves from call transcripts, #da-brain, and deal/event checklists.
2. Every item has an owner and a date — or it doesn't exist.
3. Mon + Thu (auto): the bot replays everything still open, grouped by owner.
4. You: mark done, re-date, or drop. That's the whole discipline.
